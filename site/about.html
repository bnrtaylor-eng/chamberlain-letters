The Chamberlain Letters Project is a digital edition of the correspondence of John Chamberlain (1553–1628), one of early modern England’s most dedicated letter writers. This project presents scholarly, TEI-encoded transcriptions of his letters, along with contextual notes, metadata, and linked reference entries for the people, places, and events he described.

The edition is based on Norman Egbert McClure’s 1939 publication of Chamberlain’s letters, with additions and improvements designed to make the collection more accessible, searchable, and interactive. It invites students, researchers, and interested readers to explore the social, political, and cultural life of early seventeenth-century England through the eyes of a keen and consistent observer.
